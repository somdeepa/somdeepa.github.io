Does easier access to alcohol affect road safety? Evidence from Texas municipalities

Somdeepa Das, Tong Li

Download

  • Paper
Abstract

We examine how local alcohol availability affects road safety using plausibly exogenous variation in municipal alcohol policy in Texas. Between 1990 and 2019, more than 300 Texas cities voted to legalize alcohol sales. Passage of these wet laws leads to a sizable increase in liquor establishments, with most of the increase coming from off-premise retailers. Despite that expansion in access, we find no corresponding rise in alcohol-related fatal traffic accidents, fatalities, or drunk driving in fatal accidents. The results speak to the relationship between retail access, alcohol consumption patterns, and traffic-safety externalities.
